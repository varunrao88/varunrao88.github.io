Are Developing Countries really Developing ?

This project aims to look at the world and identify the progress being made by countries across the globe. The question that we try to answer is that are developing countries showing a trend towards becoming fully developed or if it's just a dream

About the Visualization (Essay)

About the Data

The data set has been picked up from the World Bank indicators that is accessible for all. The data provides a year by year breakdown of how countries across the world are faring on various parameters. The World Bank provides an exhaustive list of parameters, for the purpose of this project I have selected a subset of these to measure the development of countries. Development is primarily measured on Socio - Economic parameters and following this train of thought I have selected 3 social parameters and 1 economic paramter to show you the narrative visualization.

The Visualization Structure

I chose the Interactive Slideshow approach. Following this page you have the option of viewing the data on a macroscopic scale to analyse the urbanization of countries from 1980 to 2014. You can then interact with the data and select countries which you want to take a deeper look into. You can also select any one the presets which is a group of countries that have already been shortlisted.

Scenes

The scenes have been kept uniform in order to add a level of coherence for the user. The graph is placed on the left side of the screen with information regarding the scenes, parameters, annotations and triggers on the right. Please look out for the navigation markers on the right to proceed. Further information about the narrative visualization is on the next page.

Visualization Structure
Scenes
The data in this scene is for almost 200 countries. We try to contrast between the change in population living in rural areas between 1980 to 2014. The premise here is that a greater percentage change indicates greater urbanization which is an Indicator of development.
Annotations
The annotations here are the tooltips on each country. The tooltip gives information regarding the population in 1980 and 2014. It also gives you the population of the country that can help you contrast the population migration vs the actual population.
Parameters
The parameters that you can select in this chart are the countries, you can select the countries you want to analyse. Clicking a country adds it to your bucket to process for further analysis. You can also click on the button to take a deep dive into your selection.
Triggers
The triggers are that if you select a country it gets added to your bucket. If you select the a continent from the legend the corresponding countries also get highlishted.
